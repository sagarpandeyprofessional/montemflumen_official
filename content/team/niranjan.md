Niranjan Dahal is a physics lecturer and researcher with a strong background in science teaching and academic research. His interests include atmospheric and upper-atmosphere physics, ionospheric Total Electron Content (TEC), and computational simulation. He brings analytical rigor and structured problem-solving to research- and education-oriented initiatives.

## Background

Niranjan has extensive experience teaching physics and science at secondary and higher-secondary levels in Kathmandu, Nepal, alongside academic research activities. His work focuses on building strong foundational understanding, fostering analytical thinking, and applying data-driven methods to scientific problems.

## Research Experience

### B.Sc. Research
**Study on construction and working principle of quadcopter (drone)**
- Constructed and studied the working principle of various physical parts of a quadcopter.

### M.Sc. Thesis & Research
**Study on Background Radiation**
- Studied and analyzed background radiation at Sisdol Landfill site (Okharpauwa, Nuwakot) using a Digital Geiger Counter (GCA-07W).

**Study on variation of Total Electron Content over Nepal**
- Studied ionospheric response to geomagnetic storms using ground-based GPS data.
- Investigated deviation of VTEC on storm days compared to quiet days.
- Studied cross-correlation of VTEC with solar wind parameters.
